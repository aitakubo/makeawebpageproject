Echeverias are very popular for their flowery shape, but are actually one of the most difficult to take care of.

They need lots of sunlight and very quick-draining soil. Echeverias can become etiolated (stretched) very easily without proper sunlight, ruining its pretty rosette shape. Given the right treatment, echeverias can be extremely prolific!

Some echeverias will produce offshoots, or mini rosettes that can be cut off to grow into their own plants. Leaf propagation and beheading are also options for propagation, but we'll go into depth about that later.

Overall, compared to other succulents echeverias are particularly:

  • sensitive to overwatering
  • need extra sunlight (full sun)
  • and easy to propagate.
